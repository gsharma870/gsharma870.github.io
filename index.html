About me

I am currently a Senior Human Machine Interface Researcher at Audi of America, Advanced Driver Assistance Systems, based in San Jose, CA. My role encompasses the entire lifecycle of user studies, from design and execution to analysis and reporting of relevant findings to stakeholders (Audi AG, Porsche AG). I work closely with cross-functional teams to translate research insights into system requirements, ensuring alignment with safety, usability, and regulatory standards. Currently, my research focus centers on evaluating the safety and usability of both semi-autonomous and fully autonomous vehicles.

My academic background lies in Computer Science and Mathematics, with a research focus on Human-Computer Interaction (HCI). Over the past 5+ years, I have conducted extensive research across diverse domains. These include investigating human interactions in physically immersive spaces, studying group dynamics in smart environments, and enhancing in-vehicle usability for semi to fully autonomous vehicles.

Within the realm of automotive Human-Machine Interface (HMI), I have also delved into topics related to Psychophysics. This involves quantifying human perception metrics for stimuli commonly employed as feedback mechanisms for drivers. For instance, I’ve explored haptic feedback via the steering wheel to provide timely warnings about proximity to road edges.
